# Chickpea and Leek Soup

![Chickpea and Leek Soup][1]{ loading=lazy }

| :fork_and_knife_with_plate: Serves | :timer_clock: Total Time |
|:----------------------------------:|:-----------------------: |
| 6 | 1.5 hours |

## :salt: Ingredients

- 12 oz (340 g) :beans: chickpeas, dried (or 2 cans 14 oz)
- :droplet: water
- 1 :sweet_potato: medium potato
- :stew: stock
- 1 Tbsp (14 g) :olive: olive oil
- 1 knob (14 g) :butter: butter
- 5 :onion: medium leeks
- 2 :garlic: garlic cloves
- :salt: salt
- 1.5 pints (850 ml) :stew: chicken or vegetable stock
- :salt: freshly ground black pepper
- :cheese_wedge: Parmesan cheese
- :olive: extra-virgin olive oil

## :cooking: Cookware

- :shallow_pan_of_food: pot
- :shallow_pan_of_food: pan
- :gear: food processor
- :gear: immersion blender
- :bowl_with_spoon: bowls

## :pencil: Instructions

### Step 1

If using dried chickpeas, rinse the soaked beans and place them in a :shallow_pan_of_food: pot. Cover with water, add the
whole peeled medium potato, and cook until tender (about ~{1 hour}). If using canned chickpeas, simmer the potato in a
little water or stock until soft.

### Step 2

In a separate thick-bottomed :shallow_pan_of_food: pan, warm the olive oil and butter. Add the sliced medium leeks and
sliced garlic cloves with a good pinch of salt. Sweat them gently over low heat until they are tender and sweet (do not
brown them).

### Step 3

Drain the cooked chickpeas and potato. Add them to the pan with the leeks and cook for ~{1 minute} to combine the
flavors.

### Step 4

Add about two-thirds of the chicken or vegetable stock to the pan. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for about
~{15 minutes}.

### Step 5

Puree exactly half of the mixture in a :gear: food processor or with an :gear: immersion blender, leaving the other half
chunky.

### Step 6

Stir the pureed half back into the pot. Add as much of the remaining stock as needed to reach your preferred
consistency. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Stir in a little grated Parmesan cheese to round
off the flavors.

### Step 7

Ladle into :bowl_with_spoon: bowls. Finish with a generous drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil, a fresh grinding of black
pepper, and an extra sprinkling of Parmesan cheese.
