# No Bake Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Energy Bites

![No Bake Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Energy Bites][1]{ loading=lazy }

| :fork_and_knife_with_plate: Serves | :timer_clock: Total Time |
|:----------------------------------:|:-----------------------: |
| 12 | 30 minutes |

## :salt: Ingredients

- :ear_of_rice: 1 cup dry oats (e.g. old fashioned)
- :coconut: 0.67 cup toasted unsweetened coconut flakes
- :chestnut: 0.5 cup peanut butter
- :seedling: 0.5 cup ground flaxseed
- :honey_pot: 0.33 cup honey
- :chocolate_bar: 0.5 cup mini chocolate chips
- :flower_playing_cards: 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- :honey_pot: some honey
- :chestnut: some peanut butter

## :cooking: Cookware

- 1 medium bowl
- 1 fridge
- 1 tablespoon
- 1 airtight container
- 1 fridge
- 1 freezer

## :pencil: Instructions

### Step 1

Add dry oats (e.g. old fashioned), toasted unsweetened coconut flakes, peanut butter, ground flaxseed, honey, mini
chocolate chips, and vanilla extract to a medium bowl and mix to combine well.

### Step 2

Place the bowl in the fridge and let it chill for 30 minutes. This resting time helps to ensure that the contents will
stick together when rolled.

### Step 3

After the chilling time, take a tablespoon of the mixture in your hand and roll it into a small ball. Repeat with
remaining oat mixture.

### Step 4

Note: If your mixture isn't sticking together after the resting period, you can add a little more honey or peanut
butter, stir well, and try rolling again. Sometimes it just needs a little more of the wet ingredients to hold together
well (the moisture in the peanut butter will depend on the brand/type that you use).

### Step 5

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks or in the freezer much longer.
