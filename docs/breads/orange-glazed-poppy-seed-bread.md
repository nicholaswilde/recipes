# Orange Glazed Poppy Seed Bread

![Orange Glazed Poppy Seed Bread][1]{ loading=lazy }

| :fork_and_knife_with_plate: Serves | :timer_clock: Total Time |
|:----------------------------------:|:-----------------------: |
| 2 loaves | 1.42 hours |

## :salt: Ingredients

- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- 2.5 cups granulated sugar
- 0.5 tsp Morton kosher salt
- 1.5 tsp baking powder
- 1.5 tbsp poppy seeds
- 3 tbsp orange zest
- 1.5 cups whole milk
- 1 cup + 2%tbsp vegetable oil
- 3 large eggs
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 tsp almond extract
- 0.75 cup granulated sugar
- 0.25 cup orange juice
- 0.5 tsp vanilla extract
- 0.5 tsp almond extract
- 2 tsp unsalted butter

## :cooking: Cookware

- 1 light-colored metal 8.5″ x 4.5″ loaf pans
- 1 parchment paper
- 1 stand mixer
- 1 medium bowl
- 1 cooling rack
- 1 small bowl

## :pencil: Instructions

### Step 1

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line two light-colored metal 8.5″ x 4.5″ loaf pans with parchment paper slings and set
aside.

### Step 2

In the bowl of a stand mixer, combine the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, Morton kosher salt, baking powder, and
poppy seeds. Stir on low speed just until combined. Sprinkle the finely zested orange zest over the top and stir on low
until evenly incorporated.

### Step 3

In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the whole milk, vegetable oil, eggs (slightly beaten), vanilla extract, and
almond extract.

### Step 4

With the mixer on low speed, slowly add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients. Beat until incorporated, then
increase the speed to medium and beat for an additional 30 seconds.

### Step 5

Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared loaf pans. Bake for 65 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the
center comes out clean. The bread should crack open lengthwise across the top; this helps the glaze soak in.

### Step 6

Transfer the loaf pans to a cooling rack.

### Step 7

While the bread is baking, whisk together the granulated sugar, orange juice, vanilla extract, almond extract, and
melted unsalted butter in a small bowl until fully incorporated to make the glaze.

### Step 8

Immediately after removing the pans from the oven, give the glaze a quick stir and brush it generously over the warm
bread tops. Use all of the glaze; it will soak in as the bread cools.

### Step 9

Let the loaves cool in the pans for about 20 minutes. Use the parchment slings to lift the bread out of the pans and
onto the cooling rack. Allow the bread to cool completely before slicing.
