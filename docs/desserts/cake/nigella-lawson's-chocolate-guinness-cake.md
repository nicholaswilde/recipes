# Nigella Lawson's Chocolate Guinness Cake

![Nigella Lawson's Chocolate Guinness Cake][1]{ loading=lazy }

| :fork_and_knife_with_plate: Serves | :timer_clock: Total Time |
|:----------------------------------:|:-----------------------: |
| 12 | 1.75 hours |

## :salt: Ingredients

- :page_facing_up: some parchment paper
- :tumbler_glass: 250 ml Guinness
- :butter: 250 g unsalted butter
- :chestnut: 75 g unsweetened cocoa powder
- :candy: 400 g caster sugar
- :baby_bottle: 150 ml sour cream
- :egg: 2 large eggs
- :flower_playing_cards: 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- :bread: 275 g all-purpose flour
- :chestnut: 2.5 teaspoons baking soda
- :cheese_wedge: 300 g cream cheese
- :candy: 150 g confectioners' sugar
- :glass_of_milk: 125 ml heavy cream

## :cooking: Cookware

- 1 9-inch springform pan
- 1 large wide saucepan
- 1 bowl
- 1 rack
- 1 platter

## :pencil: Instructions

### Step 1

Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Butter and line a 9-inch springform pan with parchment paper.

### Step 2

Pour the Guinness into a large wide saucepan, add the unsalted butter (sliced) and heat until the butter has melted.

### Step 3

Whisk in the unsweetened cocoa powder and caster sugar.

### Step 4

In a separate bowl, whisk the sour cream with the large eggs and vanilla extract, then pour into the beer-and-butter
mixture.

### Step 5

Whisk in the all-purpose flour and baking soda.

### Step 6

Pour the cake batter into the prepared pan and bake for 45 minutes to 1 hour (until a skewer comes out clean).

### Step 7

Leave to cool completely in the pan on a rack, as it is quite a damp cake.

### Step 8

To make the frosting: Lightly whip the cream cheese (room temperature) until smooth, sift over the confectioners' sugar
and then whisk them together.

### Step 9

Add the heavy cream and whisk again until it reaches a spreadable consistency.

### Step 10

Remove the cake from the pan and place on a platter. Ice the top of the cake so that it resembles the frothy head on a
pint of Guinness.
